About Norse Mythology

Norse Mythology is the ancient religion of the Vikings who came from the land of Scandinavia. Norse Mythology was originally the Norse's way of understanding the world around them, and slowly it evolved into tales of cunning and magic. Our greatest source for Norse Mythology are Snorri Sturlson's The Eddas.

Norse Mythology is used in many present day things. The days of the week deprive their names from Norse gods and goddesses and movies have been made using Norse Mythology, along with many books crafting Norse Mythology into modern-day world.

Aesir and Vanir

Aesir Gods and Goddesses

Odin or Woden: The ruler/leader of the Aesir. He is very cunning and has made many sacrifices to gain knowledge. He once hung himself from a tree for nine days to learn how to read the runes (words that can cast spells). He gives his name to Wednesday, or Woden's Day.

Thor or Porr: The god of thunder and the son of Odin. He has a hammer, Mjolnir, in which he uses to kill frost giants. His name is the origin of Thursday.

Loki: The adopted brother of Odin who is half frost giant. He causes much trouble for the Aesir and has to help them out of trouble, both of his making and of theirs.
