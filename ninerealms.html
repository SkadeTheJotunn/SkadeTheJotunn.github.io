THE NINE REALMS

The nine realms are the different worlds of Norse Mythology that are connected by the world tree, Yggdrasil. With a total of nine, the worlds are Asgard, Midgard, Alfheim, Vanaheim (my personal favorite), Niflheim, Muspelheim, Jotunheim, Svartalfheim (or sometimes Nidavellir), and finally Helheim.

Asgard

Asgard is the realm of the Aesir gods. Heimdall, the gate keeper, guards the Bifrost that connects Asgard to the other realms. Here, in Asgard, each of the Aesir have a hall in which they dwell. Odinn's is very special because, as the king, he has a throne that when he sits upon it, he can see throughput all the realms. Once, when Frey was looking for Freya, he sat upon the throne, but, rather than finding Freya, he saw the beautiful Gerd. Also, this is the realm where Valhalla is located. Valhalla is the Norse's version of warrior heaven. Valkyries sitting upon winged horses would carry the soul's of brave soldiers into Odinn's Valhalla where they would train for an eternity as they awaited the end battle of Ragnarok.

Vanaheim

Vanaheim is the realm of the Vanir gods, and its description reflects what they are associated with. It is protrayed as much more peaceful and more wild, contrasting the warrior halls of Asgard. Here, Freya hosts Folkvanger, the Vanir heaven. Fow while Odinn and his valkyries pick warriors, so does Freya, and they too prepare for Ragnarok.

About Norse Mythology

Norse Mythology is the ancient religion of the Vikings who came from the land of Scandinavia. Norse Mythology was originally the Norse's way of understanding the world around them, and slowly it evolved into tales of cunning and magic. Our greatest source for Norse Mythology are Snorri Sturlson's The Eddas.

Norse Mythology is used in many present day things. The days of the week deprive their names from Norse gods and goddesses and movies have been made using Norse Mythology, along with many books crafting Norse Mythology into modern-day world.
